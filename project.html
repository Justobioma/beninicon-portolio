An Intro on the Project Team

Chairman's Statement

The Green House Art Gallery, established in 2009, is committed to promoting Nigerian Art and Artists by showcasing their works through solo and group exhibitions, among other key objectives. In pursuing this core objective, we have been closely involved in the professional activities and growth of Princess Theresa Oghogho IyaseOdozi. Her major solo exhibitions which we have promoted during the last decade include: Arrival (2009); On Thy Roots We Stand (2012); Ounje Ale Oluwa – The Last Supper of Jesus & His 12 Disciples – (2014); and Spiritual Contemplations ( 2015) . The works displayed during these exhibitions cover mundane, abstract and spiritual dimensions, with increasing accent on the last of these endeavours. In preparation for the current exhibition, Iyase-Odozi organised an art discourse with the theme “Renaissance of Imagery and Symbolism in Benin Iconography: Iyase-Odozi's Appropriations in Focus”, as a preview of the main event. An important dimension of the appropriation of ancient Benin icons for this exhibition, is the providential discovery of the Circled Cross, embossed on brass plaques and costumes, which were in use during the time of 16th century Oba Esigie. There are many meanings and interpretations surrounding the Circled Cross in various cultures, this symbol having predated Christianity by thousands of years. It has been variously called the Sun/Solar Cross, the Dyeus Cross and Zeus Cross, among other names. Given its extensive use as a representation of Divine Truth, it is not surprising that this symbol has found its way into Christian iconography. Thus, the spiritual essence and signiﬁcance of the re-emergence of the Circled Cross, this symbol of Divine Truth, in Benin City through this exhibition, should not be lost on us. No doubt, many questions will emerge about the origins, meanings and uses of the Circled Cross and other ancient icons evident in Iyase-Odozi's art, and how they could be eﬀectively integrated into contemporary, popular culture. These are questions calling for further research. But for now, we should recognise that the reemergence of the Circled Cross, this symbol of Divine Truth, during the reign of HRM OmoN'ObaN'Edo, Uku' Akpolokpolo Ewuare II, is a good omen for Benin Kingdom. It is, indeed, our hope that this exhibition would serve to create awareness of these rich but long-forgotten icons and set aglow a renaissance of Benin iconography.

Kally Ukhaheme
